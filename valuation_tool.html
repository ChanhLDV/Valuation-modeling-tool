⚠ Disclaimer: This tool is for educational and illustrative purposes only. Outputs depend entirely on user inputs and embedded industry benchmarks (Aswath Damodaran, NYU Stern, January 2026). Multiples and risk parameters are point-in-time estimates; verify against current market data before use. This is not investment advice. Always engage a qualified valuation professional for transaction or reporting use.
Method-specific caveats: The Precedent M&A dataset is illustrative — replace with current Mergermarket / Refinitiv extracts for live work and remember transaction multiples already embed control premia. The LBO output is highly sensitive to entry leverage, debt pricing, and assumed EBITDA growth — small changes in capital structure flip returns dramatically; always stress-test. The DDM is only meaningful for mature, dividend-paying issuers with stable payout policy; do not use for growth companies that retain all earnings or for cyclical payers. Asset-Based / NAV is most relevant for holding companies, REITs, and distressed/liquidation cases — it generally undervalues going-concern operations and ignores brand, customer relationships, and other intangibles. The Football Field blend is only as reliable as the underlying method outputs — equal weights are a default, not a recommendation.

May 2026 · M&A precedent deals — illustrative composite based on public deal disclosures (Mergermarket / Refinitiv / S&P CapIQ structure).
Methodology: Comparables — EV multiples applied to TTM metrics with size/control/illiquidity adjustments. DCF — Two-stage FCFF, CAPM-based WACC, Gordon or Exit Multiple terminal value. Precedent M&A — Filtered deal universe, mean/median/percentile multiples applied to LTM metrics. LBO — Multi-tranche debt waterfall with mandatory amort and cash sweep, 3/5/7-yr exit, IRR & MoM with returns attribution (debt paydown, EBITDA growth, multiple expansion). DDM — Gordon, Two-Stage, and H-Model variants. Asset-Based / NAV — Book vs Adjusted vs Liquidation. Football Field — Auto-aggregated method ranges with user-weightable blend.
